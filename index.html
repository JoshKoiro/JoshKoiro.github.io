Why Programming?

1

Web Design/Graphic Design

My journey towards programming started with doing graphic design. I did some freelance photography, and graphic design for websites. At the time, I was not very interested in learning the programming side of things, but somewhere along the line, that changed. I began to learn JavaScript, and have not stopped since.

2

Learning JavaScript

I began teaching myself JavaScript using online tools in 2015 and have continued to do self-education on the language and frameworks for it. Specifically, NodeJS and application development frameworks like React, Angular, Meteor and even Electron. Because of the diversity of the tools and frameworks available for JavaScript, I have dabbled in many and have not settled on getting really proficient any one framework. I'm hoping getting the opportunity to code in a professional enviroment will give me an opportunity to settle on an industry standard and get very good with it.

3

Developing Tools

During the time of learning about the world of JavaScript and modern web app development, I was also seeking to apply what I was learning in the day to day of the job that I was currently working at. Unfortunately, I did not have the resources to develop NodeJS apps and deploy them for others in the company to use, so I had to find other methods. The company used SQL Server for all of their business information and Excel was the one tool that everyone had on their computers, so I used VBA to create some of the tools that the company still uses day to day.

4

Programming as a whole

More recently, I have focused on trying to understand programming as a whole. Things like understanding the difference between object-oriented and functional programming, and understanding data structures and design principles. This is the stuff that really gets me excited because it doesn’t just explain how to code and make something specific happen, but it explains why something works, or why it doesn’t. It empowers me as the learner, with the tools to be able to be creative and think outside the box.
